ii
Terrace · cicadas · espresso

The hornbills beat you to the view.

Coffee on the terrace, 50 metres above the sea. The Gulf is still waking up. A single long-tail fisherman slides out past the cape toward Sai Daeng — the only motion for miles.

Breakfast
iii
Main house · ten-seat table
09:00

Fresh mango. Thai-style eggs. No queue.

The main house holds everyone — kitchen, dining, long lounging areas. A ten-seat table for the whole family and still room for the newspaper, the toddler's drawings, and a second pot of coffee.

Underwater
iv
Our boat · SSI instructor
11:00

The dive boat leaves the pier.

Our own boats, our own SSI instructors — no third-party dive shop in between. Chumphon Pinnacles today, Shark Island reef tomorrow. Reef sharks, turtles, and a whale shark if the week is kind.

Afternoon
v
14-metre infinity pool
13:30

Shaded lounger. Nobody in the deep end.

Fourteen metres of infinity pool on the sundeck. Shaded couches, unshaded loungers, bean bags. Shallow end for the kids, deep end for the lap swimmer. Only your family on the deck.

In-villa spa
vi
Designated treatment room
15:00

The massage comes to the villa.

Eagle's main house has a designated room for in-villa massage — professional bed, therapist, complete privacy. Traditional Thai or deep tissue. No spa lobby, no drive, no robe walk-of-shame.

Golden hour
vii
180° over the Gulf
18:30

The apricot-sky moment.

One-eighty degrees of unbroken Gulf — Sai Daeng to the east, Shark Bay to the west. Sundowners on the deck. Cold glass in hand. Nobody between you and the horizon.

Dinner
viii
Private chef · Eagle Villa only
20:00

Candles. Ten seats. The 40th-anniversary toast.

Dinner cooked and served inside the villa by our private chef. Four courses, Thai or Western, your kitchen, your table. When the glasses come up for the toast, the only sound is cicadas.

Nightfall

Sundeck · lights out

No light pollution on Koh Tao.

Lights off. Loungers tilted back. The Milky Way over the Gulf — no resort glow, no neighbouring villa, no streetlamp. Kids asleep in one pavilion, the grown-ups quiet on the deck.
